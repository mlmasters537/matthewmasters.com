The Inspiration

For the Love of Type

It was about this time I had seen the documentary Abstract on Netflix after which I became a big fan of Pentagram’s Paula Scher, specifically her work on expressive informational maps. I wanted to create something like this but instead of geographical maps build a timeline on the history of typography.

The time line became so big that I starting think about designing it toward more of an environmental sense. What if this piece were placed on a wall? The viewer could literally walk through the timeline and look at the information they want. It’s almost Interactve.

Typography & Layout

The Writing on the Wall

An original goal of the History of Type project was to create the entire poster in a color style of CMYK, as print plays a huge part of the timeline. CMYK also has a great characteristic where shapes seem to retain 100% of their contrast while in a Multiply Blending mode. I did end up adding an aqua color to the mix as I felt another color was needed to break up and organize the shapes a bit more. The biggest challenge of this project was to find a balance between clarity, hierarchy, and unity. What good is a timeline if it is not clearly understood by the viewer or legible? Yet I wanted someone that looked eye-catching and made you want to approach it and see what it had to say in the first place? I believe I balanced this to points well.
