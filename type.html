The Challenge

For the Love of Print

If Fast Company is the magazine for business and Photoshop user is the technical how to authority, then Visual is somewhere in the inspiration behind design. Visual is a annual publication that explores the ideas and philosophy behind visual communications. The project came at a time when I was becoming increasingly interested in the previous work of lesser known designers and artists, like Dave McKean and Gregory Crewdson. From this came a series I called, “Ten to Inspire”, which was a leaping off point for what would later become Visual Magazine.

Visual Magazine was a project that came about in a time where I became increasing fascinated with the process and visual philosophy behind respected creative in their prospective fields. This was a grab bag of creatives ranging from graphic designers like David Carson & Chip Kidd, to illustrators like Drew Struzan & Bill Sienkiewicz to photographers Hiroshi Sugimoto & Gregory Crewdson. Visual was design to fill in the gap between instruction deigin magazines like Photoshop User and the business of design like Fast Company. It is design to inspire the reader and create a platform where the creative in their respective fields can share their ideas behind design, most importantly their process.

Editoral Layouts

Boxes, Guides & Rules

I began the project by concentrating on the companies logo as all identity systems are anchored by one. In these times, the logo must not only conform to the traditional principles of versatility like being functional on a spectrum of sizes and platforms, but in the digital age it must now be forward thinking on how they are used on digital platforms like desktop, tablet and mobile devices.

The issues that were addressed in the redesign include: odd uses of white space and kerning, closed negative spaces from the decorative scripting, legibility issues with the second half of the company name, resizing issues inherent to the design which included eliminating the “coffee roasters” banner.

I felt that a complete redesign was unecessary and would actually damage the brand if pursuited as the old logo was locally reasonably recognizable. So it became a critical goal of the redesign to retain the overall feel of the old logo it was replacing. I embraced Ozwald Boateng’s idea of, “reminiscing a bit of the past but with a forward vision.”

While there is room in this project for a more complete robust identity system, i.e. collateral, packaging, promos, etc., I felt that urgency was needed on the other problem areas and that the overall system could be addressed at a later date.
