The Challenge

Stronger Identity with a Better System

Mozart’s Coffee Roaster’s had three areas I felt they were weak in, namely, an aged identity system, a website that wasn’t responsive or mobile friendly and a large menu that suffered from what I call, information sprawl. Information sprawl is one large chunk of information spread out over multiple units of delivery or locations. I felt the right direction moving forward would be to redesign the logo, redesign from the ground up the businesses website and adding a big beautiful motion menu that contained all that they offer in one singular place.

Logo Design

A New Logo to Move Forward

I began the project by concentrating on the companies logo as all identity systems are anchored by one. In these times, the logo must not only conform to the traditional principles of versatility like being functional on a spectrum of sizes and platforms, but in the digital age it must now be forward thinking on how they are used on digital platforms like desktop, tablet and mobile devices.

The issues that were addressed in the redesign include: odd uses of white space and kerning, closed negative spaces from the decorative scripting, legibility issues with the second half of the company name, resizing issues inherent to the design which included eliminating the “coffee roasters” banner.

I felt that a complete redesign was unecessary and would actually damage the brand if pursuited as the old logo was locally reasonably recognizable. So it became a critical goal of the redesign to retain the overall feel of the old logo it was replacing. I embraced Ozwald Boateng’s idea of, “reminiscing a bit of the past but with a forward vision.”

While there is room in this project for a more complete robust identity system, i.e. collateral, packaging, promos, etc., I felt that urgency was needed on the other problem areas and that the overall system could be addressed at a later date.

Web Design

A More Mobile Friendly Website

The next step to the Mozart’s project was to re-design the businesses website, which had aged poorly. Mozart’s coffee Roasters had four main problems. First, the site was not responsive. This made mobile usage particularly challenging. Secondly, the website felt visually bolted together. It seemed too have been build over time with a bolt on method. Another issue, the UI was confusing with some pages seeming inaccessible through the site as the link were not connected with the only way on accessing these page was google searches. Finally, the site didn’t feel comprehensive wityh large chunks of information a customer might try to get missing.
