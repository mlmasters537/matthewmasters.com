The Challenge

Visualizing a Fine Wine

This was my entry into the nation-wide Canvas Wines Artisan Series design contest entitled, “The Perfect Getaway”. The goal of the competition was to design an image (excluding typography) for one of their four artisan wines - Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Cabernet Sauvignon and Pinot Grigio. The image needed to represent the flavors of the respective category you choose. I choose Pinot Grigio which had a flavor profile of light, floral, and bright.

I became fascinated during the research phase on how the flavor profile actually takes on elements of where the grapes were grown. In other words, the wine tastes like the place, time and environment in which they were grown. The flavor is literally the experince of the vineyard. I incorporated the balloon in the image to represent this idea that this wine would take you to a wonderful in place.

The Process

A New Logo to Move Forward

The biggest challenge in constructing “The Perfect Getaway”, was maintaining believable lighting consistency ( lighting overtones, direction of light, time of day, etc. ) throughout the image. Since the entire composition was composed of eight separate images and from eight different sources, simply throwing them together would not have been visually unified. Not only did this mean carefully crafting the pieces with adjustment layers and blending modes, but being aware of the lighting and shadows of the original image and gathering a set of images that worked together.
