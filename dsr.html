The Answers Hanging on the Wall

Some friends of mine decided to try their hand at running a bar in downtown Kyle, just south of Austin call Down South Rail House, also known as DSR. The bar was housed in an very old historic rail house just off the tracks and was home to some pretty awesome country music. I began working on social media promotions and printed infographics for the company with a personal challenge to not use typical country imagery like cowboy hats, boots, etc.

Light, Cameras, HDR

I began the project by concentrating on the companies logo as all identity systems are anchored by one. In these times, the logo must not only conform to the traditional principles of versatility like being functional on a spectrum of sizes and platforms, but in the digital age it must now be forward thinking on how they are used on digital platforms like desktop, tablet and mobile devices.

The issues that were addressed in the redesign include: odd uses of white space and kerning, closed negative spaces from the decorative scripting, legibility issues with the second half of the company name, resizing issues inherent to the design which included eliminating the “coffee roasters” banner.

I felt that a complete redesign was unecessary and would actually damage the brand if pursuited as the old logo was locally reasonably recognizable. So it became a critical goal of the redesign to retain the overall feel of the old logo it was replacing. I embraced Ozwald Boateng’s idea of, “reminiscing a bit of the past but with a forward vision.”

While there is room in this project for a more complete robust identity system, i.e. collateral, packaging, promos, etc., I felt that urgency was needed on the other problem areas and that the overall system could be addressed at a later date.
