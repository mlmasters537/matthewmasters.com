The Challenge

For the Love of Print

If Fast Company is the magazine for business and Photoshop user is the technical how to authority, then Visual is somewhere in the inspiration behind design. Visual is a annual publication that explores the ideas and philosophy behind visual communications. The project came at a time when I was becoming increasingly interested in the previous work of lesser known designers and artists, like Dave McKean and Gregory Crewdson. From this came a series I called, “Ten to Inspire”, which was a leaping off point for what would later become Visual Magazine.

Visual Magazine was a project that came about in a time where I became increasing fascinated with the process and visual philosophy behind respected creative in their prospective fields. This was a grab bag of creatives ranging from graphic designers like David Carson & Chip Kidd, to illustrators like Drew Struzan & Bill Sienkiewicz to photographers Hiroshi Sugimoto & Gregory Crewdson. Visual was design to fill in the gap between instruction deigin magazines like Photoshop User and the business of design like Fast Company. It is design to inspire the reader and create a platform where the creative in their respective fields can share their ideas behind design, most importantly their process.

Publication Design
Editoral

The projects goal was to design a 128 page, full color, quarterly magazine that with the idea that the viewer would not be able to tell the difference this was a personal project. The layout was design to be lightly experimental as well inspired by magazines like wired. It would be a full one-hundred page quarterly featuring advertising as well.

Advertising
Campaigns

If you’re going to design a magazine, you might as well go all the way and incorporate full advertsing campaigns to take place within it. Of course the companies and brands I choose to place into the magazine fits the market that it targets. These companies included new and old projects I had work on including Canvas wines, Microsoft Azure Platform, Clover Systems, Wacom, and other technology companies.

Editoral Layouts

Boxes, Guides & Rules

I always appreciated magazines that experimented and delivered exciting visual layouts, instead of the same old expected and safe . Visual was heavily inspired by these publications like Vanity Fair, Texas Monthly and, especially, Wired. I incorporated a lot of the latter's quality's like thin, fast reading line lengths on top of grid structures that were based on odd numbered columns. I concentrated a lot on the overall pace and tempo of the magazine, careful not to exhaust the reader buy using plenty of white space. This project also allowed me to experiment with balancing expressive formats and more modernist aesthetics.

Advertisements

Going the Extra Mile

If you’re going to create a magazine, why not go all the way and create everything that would go in one? I opted to create a group of advertainments to accompany the magazine that targeted what I believed the core audience of the publication was, namely mid-twenties to thirties working professional in the field of Visual Communications.

Each advertisement was a brand-new direction for the company I covered. I did not want to mimic the campaign or visual style that company was currently doing. I covered single one off, two-page spreads and even a double gate fold for Microsoft’s Azure cloud service. Other companies covered in the magazine include Canvas wines, Microsoft Azure Platform, Clover Systems, Wacom, Rockstar, Trino, Franklin Print, etc.
