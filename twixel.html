The Challenge

Cutting Out the Noise

The gaming sector is a massive industry with, as of 2015, revenue of $91.5 billion dollars internationally. To put this number in perspective - gaming has doubled the film industry globally. Unfortunately, you wouldn’t be able tell this by looking at the majority of popular indie content surrounding it. Some of the most popular gamer-centric video channels like YouTube and streaming services like Twitch, can be visually underwhelming. They are typically, one man shows that give little thought to presentation and more weight toward a regularity and speedy delivery of content. I believed that this was achievable while maintaining a quality presentation with a strong identity. Tackling this design challenge became the cornerstone of Twixel. This Project covered three key areas of the streamer/content-creators content: You-Tube presentation, OBS/Twitch, traditional social media (Facebook, Twitter, Etc.) and a website to tie the package together.

Broadcast Streaming
Stronger Indentity

Streaming has become a necessity. Platforms like YouTube, Twitch and Mixxer are foundational to gamer centric audiences. I created a modular, fully realized broadcast system of multimedia graphics using OBS that allows the host to easily and quickly change the format of their broadcast of the fly.

Video On Demand
Faster production pipeline

The creation of flexible modules for video content is key when trying to retain a high bar of qaulity with channels run by small groups or individuals. Designs were created to be flexible and easily swapped or modified giving the user faster output to compete with bigger channels.

Web Design
Community Hub

I strongly believe that content creators shying away from maintaining a hub website is a mistake. It leads to information sprawl over multiple social media platforms that only give a slice of information. The addition of a Hub website has all the information on it and helps connect fans to other social media accounts.

Logo Design

A New Logo to Move Forward

I began the project by concentrating on the companies logo as all identity systems are anchored by one. In these times, the logo must not only conform to the traditional principles of versatility like being functional on a spectrum of sizes and platforms, but in the digital age it must now be forward thinking on how they are used on digital platforms like desktop, tablet and mobile devices.

The issues that were addressed in the redesign include: odd uses of white space and kerning, closed negative spaces from the decorative scripting, legibility issues with the second half of the company name, resizing issues inherent to the design which included eliminating the “coffee roasters” banner.

I felt that a complete redesign was unecessary and would actually damage the brand if pursuited as the old logo was locally reasonably recognizable. So it became a critical goal of the redesign to retain the overall feel of the old logo it was replacing. I embraced Ozwald Boateng’s idea of, “reminiscing a bit of the past but with a forward vision.”

While there is room in this project for a more complete robust identity system, i.e. collateral, packaging, promos, etc., I felt that urgency was needed on the other problem areas and that the overall system could be addressed at a later date.

Broadcast System

Easy, Fast Streaming Manipulation

Twixel videos are set up with a range of easy to use and fast motion graphic stingers and title cards in order to keep production flow as fast as possible. This was key target as most video content crated on YouTube are created by small 1-2 persons.

Real-time broadcasting using services like Twitch, while not as popular as streaming platforms like YouTube, is key to certain massive thriving communities, particularly in the gamer subculture. Much the modular designs created for fast, efficient video production, the title cards, transitions and stingers were all created to be as simple and user friendly as possible. There are nine cards in total, all design to be used around the num-pad, keys 1-9. This includes: Custom holding and outro cards (specific to the game being played), a stream over credits, a break card, a pre-roll, and a video intro.
