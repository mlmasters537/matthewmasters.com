The Challenge

Stronger Identity with a Better System

My Fit Foods is a local Austin chain of stores that provide healthy meals for people with busy lifestyles. All of their meals are made with healthy, fresh ingredients and is packaged and prepare for convenience. The idea behind the company was brilliant, but I felt that its underthought identity made the brand feel a bit generic and cheap. I set myself to the task of creating a new look for My Fit Foods that would elevate the brands perception and also worked on a new advertising direction that would help it stand out.

Logo Design

A Better Fit for the Brand

The original 2013 logo for My Fit Foods projected a brand identity that was overly whimsical. I felt it made the company, a medium sized corporation, feel small. It visually did not introduce consumer confidence in it. Some of the elements to the Stick Man seemed to unnecessarily champion 50% of the market.

The 2015 logo refresh seemingly to go in the exact opposite direction of visual problems. The logo was designed to clean and modernist, but in doing so scrubbed all personality from the company.

I felt that a happy middle between something with personality yet still clean would be achievable. The new version would concentrate on the ideas of “strong”, “lean” and “friendly”. Informational Layout

A Complete Corporate System

The big chunk of the project was creating a full corporate identity guide for My Fit Foods. Visually I kept the layout very clean for clarity and bite sized for fast reading. The guide includes all information a designer coming on to the My Fit Foods Team would need including rationale of the process. The guide also includes various mockups of various stationary items, advertising and ephemera.

Advertising

Whimsical & Eye Catching

The original advertising campaign for My Fit Foods championed the weekend warrior. The marathon runner, the yoga, fitness freaks and enthusiast. It utilized images of them. After showing the designs to various designers I was met with a mild response. I knew I had to turn the volume up on the idea and went back to the drawing board.

I came up with the idea to take the idea of “Fit Foods” literally. To have various animals mimicking the exercises and daily routines of the target audience. This idea created images that were eye catching and playfully stood out among other competitors.
